Let $\nu$ be a [Borel](https://mathgloss.github.io/MathGloss/Borel_Ïƒ-algebra) [measure](https://mathgloss.github.io/MathGloss/measure_space) on $\mathbb R^n$ (i.e. the underlying [measure space](https://mathgloss.github.io/MathGloss/measure_space) is $(\mathbb R^n, \mathcal B, \nu)$). The **symmetric derivative** of $\nu$ at $x \in \mathbb R^n$ is $$(D\nu)(x) = \lim_{r\to 0} \frac{\nu(B(x,r))}{\mu(B(x,r))}$$ if this limit exists.
