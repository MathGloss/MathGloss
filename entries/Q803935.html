Baire function

MathGloss Definition

The Baire classes of continuous real-valued functions on a topological space $X$ are defined as follows:

  • continuous functions are Baire-0;
  • functions that are the pointwise limit of a sequence of continuous functions are Baire-1;
  • for all $\alpha \in \mathbb N$, functions that are the pointwise limit of a sequence functions of Baire class less than $\alpha$ are Baire-$\alpha$.
