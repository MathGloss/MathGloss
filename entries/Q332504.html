Absolute continuity

MathGloss Definition

A function $f:a,b]\to \mathbb R^n$ is absolutely continuous if for every $\varepsilon > 0$, there exists $\delta > 0$ such that if $\{(x_i,y_i)\}_{i=1}^k$ are pairwise disjoint sub[intervals of $[a,b]$ with total combined length less than $\delta$, then $$\sum_{i=1}^k \vert f(y_i) - f(x_i)\vert < \varepsilon.$$
