symplectic Lie algebra

MathGloss Definition

Let $V$ be a finite-dimensional vector space with $\text{dim}(V)=2\ell$ with basis $\{v_1,\dots,v_{2\ell}\}$. Define a nondegenerate skew-symmetric form $f$ on $V$ with the matrix $$s = \begin{bmatrix}0 & I_\ell \\ -I_\ell & 0\end{bmatrix}.$$ Note that the dimension being even is required for the existence of a nondegenerate bilinear form satisfying $f(v,w) = -f(w,v)$. Then denote by $\mathfrak{sp}(V)$ the symplectic algebra consisting of all endomorphisms $x$ of $V$ such that $f(x(v),w) = -f(v,x(w)).$
