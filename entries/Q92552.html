Galois theory

MathGloss Definition

An element algebraic over the field $F$ can be solved for by radicals if it is an element of a field $K$ which can be obtained as a succession of simple extensions adjoining $n$th roots of elements of $F$. A polynomial $f(x) \in F[x]$ can be solved by radicals if all of its roots can be solved *for* by radicals.
