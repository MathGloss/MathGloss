MathGloss Definition

Let $z\in \mathbb C$ be a complex number. Then the argument of $z$, denoted $\text{arg}(z)$, is the angle between the vector in $\mathbb R^2$ corresponding to $z$ and the positive real axis. Equivalently, it is any real number $\theta$ such that $z= r(\cos\theta+i\sin\theta) = re^{i\theta}$, via Euler's formula.
