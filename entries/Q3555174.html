eigenvector

MathGloss Definition

Let $T:V\to V$ be a linear transformation from the vector space $(V,F)$ to itself. Then $v \in V$ is an eigenvector of $T$ if there exists $\lambda\in F$ such that $T(v) = \lambda v$.
