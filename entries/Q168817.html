algebraic number

MathGloss Definition

An algebraic number is a number that is a root of a nonzero polynomial in one variable over $\mathbb Q$. It is often denoted by $\overline{\mathbb Q}$. The algebraic numbers form a field, but *not* a subfield (nor even a subset) of $\mathbb Q$.
