Transcendental number

MathGloss Definition

Let $F$ be a field and let $E$ be a field extension of $F$. Let $\Theta \in E$ and consider a nonzero polynomial $f\in F[x]$. If $f(\Theta)\neq 0$, then $\Theta$ is transcendental over $F$.

Equivalently, an element of a field is transcendental if it is not algebraic.
