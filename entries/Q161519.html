permutation

MathGloss Definition

If $\sigma \in S_n$ is an element of the symmetric group on $n$ letters, then it is the product of disjoint cycles of length $n_1\leq\cdots\leq n_r$ (including the $1$-cycles!) and moreover the sum of the $n_i$ is equal to $n$. The partition of $n$ given by the $n_i$ is called the cycle type of $\sigma$.
