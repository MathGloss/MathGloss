algebra homomorphism

MathGloss Definition

Let $A$ and $B$ be two algebras over the same field (or commutative ring) $K$. Then an algebra homomorphism between $A$ and $B$ is a map $f:A\to B$ such that 1. $f(kx) = kf(x)$ 2. $f(x+y) = f(x) + f(y)$ 3. $f(xy) = f(x)f(y)$ for all $k\in K$ and $x,y\in A$.

If an algebra homomorphism is bijective, then it is an isomorphism of algebras.
