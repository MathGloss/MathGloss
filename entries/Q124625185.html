Young subgroup

MathGloss Definition

Let $\lambda = \{\lambda_1,\dots,\lambda_k\}$ be a partition. Let $\alpha_i = \{\lambda_i, \lambda_i + 1, \dots, \lambda_{i+1} - 1\}$ so that $\{1,\dots,n\} = \coprod_{i=1}^k \alpha_i$ as disjoint subsets. Corresponding to this decomposition is the Young subgroup of the symmetric group $S_n$ given by $$S_{\alpha_1} \times \cdots \times S_{\alpha_k}$$ where $S_{\alpha_i} = \{\sigma\in S_n \mid \sigma(j) = j \text{ for all } j\notin \alpha_i\}$.
