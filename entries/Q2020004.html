Galois extension

MathGloss Definition

A field extension $E/F$ is Galois if it is algebraic, normal, and separable. The group of automorphisms of $E$ that fix $F$, denoted $\text{Gal}(E/F)$, is called the Galois group of $E$ over $F$.

Equivalently, $E$ is Galois over $F$ if the order of the automorphism group $\text{Aut}(E/F)$ is equal to the degree of $E$ over $F$.
