MathGloss Definition

A vector space $V$ over the field $F$ is a set together with two operations "addition" ($+: V\times V \to V$) and "scalar multiplication" ($\cdot: F\times V \to V$) satisfying the following eight axioms: 1. Addition is associative 2. Addition is commutative 3. There exists a vector $0 \in V$ such that $0 + v = v$ for all $v \in V$. ($0$ is the additive identity in $V$) 4. For every $v \in V$, there exists $-v \in V$ such that $v + (-v) = 0$. ($-v$ is the additive inverse of $v$) 5. $a(bv)= (ab)v$ for all $a,b \in F$ and $v \in V$. 6. $1v = v$ for all $v \in V$, where $1$ is the multiplicative identity in $F$. 7. $a(u+v) = au+av$ for all $a \in F$, $u,v \in V$. 8. $(a+b)v = av+bv$ for all $a,b \in F$, $v\in V$.

Axioms 7. and 8. are distributive laws.
