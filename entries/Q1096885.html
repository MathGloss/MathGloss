MathGloss Definition

A lattice is a partially ordered set in which every pair of elements has a unique infimum and a unique supremum.

If $(X, \leq)$ is a lattice, we call the supremum of $x$ and $y$ the join of $x$ and $y$ and denote it $x\lor y$. Similarly, we call the infimum of $x$ and $y$ their meet and denote it $x\land y$. These are in fact binary operations on $X$. Moreover, they respect the partial order on $X$.

We can also define a lattice as a triple $(L, \lor,\land)$ where $L$ is a set and $\lor$ and $\land$ are two commutative, associative binary operations on $L$ satisfying the following absorption laws for all $a,b \in L$: 1. $a\lor (a\land b) = a$; 2. $a\land (a\lor b) = a$.
