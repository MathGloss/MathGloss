absolute convergence

MathGloss Definition

Let $A$ be a Banach algebra with norm $\vert \cdot \vert$. Given a sequence $\{a_i\}_{i\in \mathbb N}$ of elements of $A$, the series $$\sum_{i=1}^\infty a_i$$ is absolutely convergent if there exists $L \in\mathbb R$ such that $$\sum_{i=1}^\infty \vert a_i\vert =L.$$

This means that the partial sums $$\sum_{i=1}^n a_i$$ form a Cauchy sequence in $A$, and since $A$ is complete by definition, the sequence must have a limit in $A$. We denote this limit by the infinite sum.
