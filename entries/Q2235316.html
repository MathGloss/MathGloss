Elementary symmetric function

MathGloss Definition

Let $R$ be a ring and let $R[x_1,\dots,x_n]$ be the polynomial ring in $n$ variables. Then the elementary symmetric polynomials are $$\begin{align*}e_1(x_1,\dots,x_n) &= \sum_{1\leq j\leq n} x_j \\ e_2(x_1,\dots,x_n) &= \sum_{1\leq j < k \leq n} x_jx_k \\e_3(x_1,\dots, x_n) &=\sum_{1\leq j < k < \ell\leq n} x_jx_kx_\ell\\&\quad\vdots\\e_n(x_1,\dots,x_n) &= x_1x_2\cdots x_n.\end{align*}$$ In general, $$e_k(x_1,\dots,x_n) = \sum_{1\leq j_1<j_2<\cdots < j_k \leq n} x_{j_1}\cdots x_{j_k}$$ for $1\leq k\leq n$. They are symmetric polynomials.
